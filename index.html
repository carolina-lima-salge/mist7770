Course Description

This course provides a broad understanding of technologies and processes for gathering, storing, accessing, and analyzing data to help business users make better decisions. Topics include data science and storytelling with data; R, RStudio, R Markdown, and Tableau; wrangling and webscraping; visualization, transformation, and machine learning. These topics are divided into four sections: (1) data landscape and tools, (2) data gathering and pre-processing, (3) data analysis and presentation.


This course uses the Hybrid Synchronous instructional approach. Under this approach, a certain number of students (based on classroom capacity) join the instructor on a rotating basis for socially-distanced, face-to-face instruction during each class session, with remaining students joining synchronously through Zoom during the assigned class time. Students are encouraged to attend class in-person on their designated days. Information about your assigned cohort (Tuesday or Thursday) is available on eLC (Welcome Announcement).


Please note that the instructional approach may change as need warrants.

Objectives

Students completing this course will be able to:

  • Understand the basics of data science and machine learning
  • Import, wrangle, transform, visualize, and model data in RMarkdown
  • Use R and RStudio to scrape data from the web
  • Become familiar with Tableau for data science
  • Leverage storytelling for presenting data insights
